Max Homa is an American professional golfer who plays on the PGA Tour. Homa was born in Burbank, California.

He attended Valencia High school in Santa Clarity Valley. He then furthered to the university of California on scholarship where he played golf. He completed earning a degree in consumer behaviour.

There, as a junior from 2011 to 12 Homa was a third-team PING Division I All-American and second-team All-Pac-12 and PING Division I All-West Region.

As a senior in 2012-13, he was ranked No. 19 nationally by Golfweek (5/19) and No. 22 according to Golfstat (5/21), was first-team All-Pac-12, ranked No. 17 on the final Palmer Cup Ranking, and won the Pac-12 Championship with an opening-round nine-under-par 61 breaking the course record at the North Course at Los Angeles Country Club and tying for the lowest round posted in the U.S.

He was named as part of the first-team All Americans and the All Nicklaus Team by the Golf Association Of America.

He is Jewish. In December 2018, he tweeted: “Despite 6 years of Hebrew school and the completion of my Bar Mitzvah, the most Jewish I’ve ever felt came after looking at a home with extravagant Christmas lights and immediately thinking “that electric bill must be brutal”.

In November 2019, he married Lacey Croom, and the couple lives in Scottsdale, Arizona with their dog, Scotty.
