Early Life

Leonardo DiCaprio was born Leonardo Wilhelm DiCaprio on November 11th, 1974, in Los Angeles, California, United States.

He is the only child of father George DiCaprio, an underground comic writer, distributor, and publisher of comic books.

His father, George, is of German and Italian ancestry. However, his maternal grandfather Wilhelm Indenbirken was German. Similarly, his maternal grandmother was a Russian-born German citizen.

Leo was raised Catholic.

His parents met each other while attending college and moved to LA after graduating.

Leo was named after Leonardo Da Vinci because his pregnant mother was looking at a Da Vinci painting in the Uffizi museum in Florence, Italy when he kicked for the first time.

His parents divorced when he was just one. Even though they initially agreed to live next door to each other, DiCaprio would have his father’s presence.

Nevertheless, DiCaprio’s mother later moved around numerous neighborhoods in LA.

Leo attended Seeds Elementary School and later joined John Marshall High School after attending the Los Angeles Center for Enriched Studies for four years.

DiCaprio hated public school and often asked his mother to take him to auditions to improve their financial situation.

He dropped out of high school but got his GED later.
