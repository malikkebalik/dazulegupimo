As of his career, Pop Smoke began his start in music in 2018 while hanging out with other recording artists in the studio. He got his start in the drill music scene of New York City by remixing famous songs before moving on to create original music, which propelled him to popularity.

Pop Smoke is a mix of Papa (a nickname given to him by his Panamanian grandmother) and Smoke Oh Guap, he said in a Genius interview (a nickname given to him by childhood friends). “Welcome to the Party,” the lead track off his debut mixtape, Meet the Woo, was released in April 2019. (2019).

In August 2019, the song was remixed with Nicki Minaj and Skepta. The song was notable for its use of UK drill production, which would subsequently become a staple of his repertoire. “Mpr,” “Flexin,” and “Dior” are some of his other tunes.

Following the success of “Welcome to the Party,” he teamed with other well-known musicians on tracks including “War” with Lil Tjay and “100k on a Coupe” with Calboy. He was featured on “Gatti” alongside JackBoys and Travis Scott, which was included on Scott and fellow Cactus Jack bandmates’ JackBoys (2019) album.

In February 2020, he released his second mixtape, Meet the Woo 2, which included Quavo, A Boogie wit da Hoodie, Fivio Foreign, and Lil Tjay. A deluxe edition was released the next week, with three additional songs: “Wolves” with Nav, Dior (Remix) with Gunna, and “Like Me” with PnB Rock.

On the 17th of January 2020, he was detained at John F. Kennedy International Airport in New York on a charge of transferring a stolen automobile across state borders. On the same day, he pled not guilty and was freed on a $250,000 bond.
